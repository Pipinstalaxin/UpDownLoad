Course Questions

Question 1
Have been through all the set up videos and am not clear on the channel settings.
1) Is the offset of 1 a percentage? For example the offset of 1 of the simple 2 period MA of the highs is 1% above the 2 period MA of the highs.
2) I had expected that the offset of the 2 period MA of the lows would be -1, i.e. in the opposite direction to the MA of the highs otherwise it would appear that the system could be biased towards short positions. Am I missing a point here?

Answer 1
1. No, Most charting software does do this now but it means simply moving the average one place to the right, not a %. Imagine you placed a pieces of tracing paper on top of your chart and drew the average on that piece of paper. Then imagine you simply move that average slightly over to the right. Alternatively, imagine calculating a 2 period MA in an Excel sheet, then moving that whole column up 1 for a displacement of 1.
2. There is no bias in either direction. I think if you understand point one then that might help.

Have a look at this PDF lesson HERE (Right Click And Save Target As).

Question 2
You are saying that important thing is to wait for all trends (long, medium, short) to be in the same direction. What do you do when all three are already moving in the same direction? Do you wait for the short-term chart to pullback to fib levels?

Can you clarify how to enter a trade? If you want to buy, are you waiting for the 20 EMA of the close to move above the 21 EMA of the open? Or are you waiting for the price to move above the upper channel (displaced MA of the high) on the short term chart?

Answer 2
Yes, the perfect situation is for all three trends (time periods) to be in buy or sell at the same time as determined by the 20/21 averages. Let's assume that the long term chart is in buy, if you are just starting with the method I would suggest to wait for the market to pull back to a fib level on the long term chart. The 20/21 should still remain in buy even if there is a close below the lower channel line. So we have this situation. The long term trend pulls back to a fib level and then you start to monitor the medium and short term trends.

Now, depending on what you are observing you may decided that when the short tern trend starts to turn up e.g. the 20/21 are in buy and the closes are above the lower channel line then that might be enough to trigger the trade. If you are unsure you might want to wait for the medium term trend to also move into buy. In summary - for a long trade you need some kind of retracement in the long term time period. Once that retracements start to show signs of support, you start to look at the medium and short term trend to give you an indication that the market has indeed found support and is startling it way back up.

If the Long tern trend is in buy and the medium term trend is also in buy you can simply wait for retracements in the short term trend using fibs or the channel to enter a trade.
